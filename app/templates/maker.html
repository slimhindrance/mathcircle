I build software that makes sense of the mess.

Base2ML

I'm the founder of Base2ML, a small AI and data engineering practice. I take on a few engagements at a time — the kind where the goal is a real working system that makes a hard decision easier for the people who have to make it.

Work I've done over the last six years:

  • Computer-vision systems for transportation and law enforcement
  • Data pipelines for the Department of Defense
  • Document-processing systems for VA healthcare
  • A long tail of business-automation work for small companies that needed something specific and didn't want a platform

I also teach part-time at Georgia Tech as an instructional associate in machine learning.

How I think about the work

I'd rather build the smallest thing that proves an idea is good and let it grow from there. Boring infrastructure when boring works, AI where AI actually adds something a human notices.

Where I am

I came back to Pittsburgh after a few years in DC. I'm raising a family here because the community reflects the values I grew up with — neighborliness, education, compassion, the long view.

Outside of work I like throwing parties and hunting the big woods.
