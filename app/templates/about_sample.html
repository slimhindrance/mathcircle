For parents who want their kids to "get ahead"

Most "ahead" tools accelerate arithmetic: a Grade 1 child solving Grade 3 problems. That's a kind of ahead, but it's a shallow one — and research suggests it can actively hurt deeper mathematical thinking later.

This app is a different kind of ahead. A child finishing K with Math Circle Home will know less arithmetic than a kid drilled on flashcards — but will know more about how numbers work: that 7 = 5 + 2 = 4 + 3 = 10 - 3, that an equation balances both sides, that a pattern has a rule, that you can argue about an answer. Those skills are what predict mathematical success in middle school and beyond.

We call it depth before acceleration. The samples below are real sessions — pulled live from the bank — at three difficulty bands. Read them as a parent would: could my child have a real conversation about this?

What's missing from this preview

The static page above shows the content. The real app adds:

  • Adaptive difficulty per strand. A child fluent at patterns but stuck on geometry sees harder patterns and easier geometry. The system targets productive confusion, not get-it-right.
  • Strategy notes. The parent or child types in how they solved it. Over weeks, you can see when "count up" gives way to "decompose to ten" — the moment that signals real growth.
  • Parent ratings. Three taps: easy / good struggle / too hard. The system listens.
  • Hints that reveal progressively. Three tiers of scaffolding. Children build the habit of trying first, asking second.
  • JSON/CSV export. Every attempt, every strategy note. Yours forever.
  • Math Circle Night mode. Cooperative sessions for siblings or play-dates.
